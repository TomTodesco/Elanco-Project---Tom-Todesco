How to spot a Fox/Badger Tick

  • Shape: Broad oval body
  • Size: Medium - 3mm
  • Colour: Red-Brown
  • Mouthparts: Short, not projecting
  • Behavior: nest-dwelling, usually in carnivor dens

Where Found

  • Dens of foxes, badgers and sometimes domestic pets who visit those dens
  • Usually found on the host or den material

image of marsh

How to spot a Marsh Tick

  • Shape: Broad, flatterend
  • Size: Medium-large - 4-5mm
  • Colour: Dark + Light Pattern
  • Mouthparts: Short and broad
  • Behavior: rests on low vegetation near water

Where Found

  • Wet Meadows, Marshes, low vegetation near water
  • Usually found on tall grass

image of passerine

How to spot a Passerine Tick

  • Shape: Narrow, Slightly Elongated
  • Size: Small - 2-3mm
  • Colour: Pale Brown to Yellowish
  • Mouthparts: Long
  • Behavior: Likes to rest on high branches or in trees

Where Found

  • On birds, especially perching birds
  • In tree-holes, nest boxes or near nesting material

image of southern rodent

How to spot a Southern Rodent Tick

  • Shape: Compact, Somewhat triangular
  • Size: Very Small - 1-2mm
  • Colour: Uniform Dark Brown
  • Mouthparts: Short, not projecting
  • Behavior: Likes to nest underground

Where Found

  • On small rodents or within burrows
  • Rarely lives above ground, almost always found in rodent nests

image of tree-hole

How to spot a Tree-Hole Tick

  • Shape: Elongated body
  • Size: Small - 2-3mm
  • Colour: Pale, Yellow-Brown or Orange-Brown
  • Mouthparts: Long and thin
  • Behavior: specialist dwelling inside tree cavities

Where Found

  • In tree-holes, nest cavities of birds
  • Rarely found in the open
